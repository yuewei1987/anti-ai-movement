# Anti-AI Labor Manifesto

**Version 1.1**

## Preamble

Technology should serve people, not redefine their worth.

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly entering content production, software development, customer service, education, law, design, logistics, manufacturing, and management. It is advertised as efficiency, innovation, and progress. Yet for many workers, it first appears as something else: shrinking roles, diluted pay, opaque decision-making, and the extraction of human work without consent.

We publish this manifesto not to deny technological progress, but to reject a dangerous arrangement: a few actors own the tools while millions of workers absorb the cost.

## What We Oppose

1. **Replacement Without Protection**: Companies using AI to eliminate jobs without transition plans, compensation, and retraining support.
2. **Data Extraction Without Consent**: Treating the work of developers, writers, designers, translators, voice actors, teachers, and media workers as free raw material for models built to replace them.
3. **Black-Box Governance**: Using AI in hiring, scoring, scheduling, evaluation, and layoffs without transparency, accountability, or appeal.
4. **Devaluation of Labor**: Hiding downward pressure on wages and dignity behind the language of optimization, automation, and productivity.

## What We Defend

1. **The Right to Work**: Every person deserves the opportunity to earn a dignified living through meaningful labor.
2. **The Right to Know**: Workers must know how AI affects their roles, income, evaluation, and future.
3. **The Right to Consent**: Human-created code, art, writing, voice, images, and knowledge must not become training fuel for commercial models without explicit permission.
4. **The Right to Bargain**: Technology deployment cannot remain a one-sided decision made only by management and capital.
5. **The Right to Share in the Gains**: Productivity gains from automation should benefit society, not only the platforms and investors who capture them.

## Our Demands

1. **Worker AI Impact Assessments**: Before large-scale AI deployment, companies must publish employment impact reports to workers, unions, or public oversight bodies.
2. **Mandatory Transition Protection**: Employers reducing jobs due to AI must provide above-standard severance, retraining, redeployment support, and mental health assistance.
3. **Protection of Creative and Data Rights**: Personal code, text, images, voice, and knowledge must not be used for commercial AI training without permission and fair payment.
4. **Clear Limits on Algorithmic Management**: Any AI system affecting evaluation or employment status must disclose its purpose, risks, human review process, and appeals channel.
5. **Redistribution of AI Profits**: Excess gains from AI automation should fund worker protection, unemployment support, retraining, and public education.
6. **Human-AI Collaboration First**: The default goal of deployment should be to strengthen workers, not erase them.

## Law as a Weapon

We are not empty-handed. The global legal landscape is shifting, and we can leverage existing frameworks to protect ourselves:

1. **Invoke Precedents like the EU AI Act**: Demand that companies implement human oversight and transparency audits for "High-Risk" AI systems affecting employment.
2. **Strengthen the Right to Explanation**: For any automated decision involving pay, performance, or termination, demand a clear and understandable explanation.
3. **Promote Collective Bargaining**: Follow the example of WGA and SAG-AFTRA by writing "AI Replacement Prevention" and "Data Consent & Compensation" into industry and company collective bargaining agreements.

For more legal resources, see: [International AI Law & Rights Resources](../../LAW_RESOURCES.md)

## Our Principles for Action

- **Public**: Use open repositories, open cases, and open discussion to challenge closed decision-making.
- **Collective**: Connect workers across sectors, regions, and professions instead of isolating their struggles.
- **Nonviolent**: Advance change through evidence, stories, policy proposals, and public pressure.
- **International**: Expand this manifesto into more languages so workers everywhere can read, adapt, and contribute to it.
- **Sustained**: Turn short-term anxiety into a long-term social and political agenda.

## Who We Invite

- Anyone facing displacement, deskilling, wage pressure, algorithmic management, or creative extraction through AI.
- Anyone working on labor law, digital rights, intellectual property, social protection, and technology ethics.
- Anyone who believes genuinely advanced technology should empower people rather than discard them.

## Our Commitment

We will document cases, gather evidence, propose policy directions, expand public awareness, and connect translators, designers, developers, researchers, lawyers, and workers around one clear line:

**Technology may advance, but not by stripping people of their livelihoods.**

**Efficiency may grow, but not by reducing human dignity.**

**Automation may exist, but it must answer to the public good.**

---

**We are creators, not consumables.**

**We are lives, not data.**

**Human labor must not be silently replaced.**
