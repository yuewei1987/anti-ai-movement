# International AI Law & Worker Rights Resources

This page summarizes key regulations, policies, and legal frameworks worldwide regarding AI regulation, worker protection, and algorithmic transparency. Use these as tools to defend your rights.

---

## 1. Core Legal Frameworks

### 🇪🇺 EU AI Act (2024)
*The world's first comprehensive legal framework for AI.*
- **Risk-Based Approach**: Categorizes AI systems by risk level.
- **High-Risk Systems**: AI used in **employment, human resources management (e.g., recruitment, termination, promotion)** is classified as "High-Risk." These systems must adhere to strict transparency, data governance, and human oversight obligations.
- **Prohibited Practices**: Bans emotion recognition in workplaces and untargeted scraping of facial images.
- **Action Point**: Workers have the right to receive explanations for automated decisions affecting their status.

### 🇪🇺 EU Platform Work Directive (2024)
- **Core Clause**: Prohibits dismissals based solely on algorithmic decisions.
- **Human Oversight**: Mandates human monitoring of algorithmic management and gives workers the right to contest automated decisions.

---

## 2. International Guidelines

### 🇺🇳 International Labour Organization (ILO)
- **Human-Centric Approach**: Emphasizes that AI deployment must follow a human-centric approach, ensuring digital transformation does not undermine fundamental labor rights.
- **Collective Bargaining**: Encourages using Collective Bargaining Agreements (CBAs) to manage career transitions caused by AI.

---

## 3. Specific Regional Practices

### 🇪🇸 Spain's "Rider Law"
- Requires companies to disclose the logic of algorithms affecting working conditions to labor unions.

### 🇺🇸 USA (State-Level)
- **New York City (Local Law 144)**: Requires bias audits for automated employment decision tools used in hiring.
- **California/New York State**: Moving toward requiring disclosure of mass layoffs caused by AI.

### 🇺🇸 Collective Bargaining Successes
- **WGA/SAG-AFTRA (Hollywood)**: Secured contract clauses preventing AI replacement and requiring consent/compensation for digital replicas.
- **Culinary Workers Union (Las Vegas)**: Won the right to advance notice of AI implementation and guaranteed severance/retraining for affected workers.

---

## 4. Legal Rights You Can Demand

1. **Right to Know**: You have the right to know which workplace decisions are being made by algorithms.
2. **Right to Explanation**: You have the right to demand an explanation of the logic behind automated decisions affecting your livelihood (e.g., layoffs, pay cuts).
3. **Right to Human Intervention**: Any significant personnel decision must involve a meaningful human review.
4. **Data Sovereignty**: Your labor output should not be used to train models designed to replace you without your consent and fair compensation.
